Upon turning right, the posture of every crewmate is aligned as soon as the sun warms their backs. Time passes as you maintain your sight on what borders the glistening water and the nearly cloudless sky. The man in the crow’s nest shouts “Land Ho!” as he points slightly towards the starboard. The crew huddles close to the bow as a dark mass appears over the water. You climb up to the deck to get a better look, while the crew whispers amongst themselves. One of the crewmates, out of the ordinary, exclaims “Savage land! We must never journey there!” and starts running towards the left side of the ship to jump. Luckily, a loose rope trips his foot and he falls to the floor. As he tries to recover, one other fellow crewmate apprehends him. He squirms and stirs under his much bigger mate’s grip upon him, until eventually another crewmate knocks him out with a bucket. Your first mate orders them to place him in the brig. While they carry him, your first mate tells you: “Captain, we haven’t felt the sand in a while. It may be good to stop somewhere and just rest and regather what we know about our current situation. Do you agree?”

  • Steer away in the opposite direction.
