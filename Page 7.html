White Flag

With haste, your crew hoists the White Flag up the pole as you signal your surrender. As your ship cruises, the black ship turns until its bow points in your direction. You order the first mate to continue the course, and the smaller sails go up to slow your ship’s speed. “I recommend you stay by your quarters, Captain” says the first mate. Ignoring him, you walk down to the bow of your own ship and stare into the distance, where a telescope aboard the dark-sailed ship reflects the sunlight into your eyes. Eventually, the pirates slow to the port side of the ship and board. As a plank is dropped between the two ships, your crew stands on the deck with shaky knees and wide eyes. The pirates only look slightly sloppier than they do, and two cross the plank onto your ship. The crew aboard the dark ship make a path to the plank as a coated figure crosses. His beard is gray and long, barnacles were growing on the tail of his hat and coat, but his hook blinds everyone with its silver shine. “You the Cap’n?” he asks you, and you look behind you to realize your crew has retreated to the starboard side of the ship, opposite of where the pirates boarded, while you stand a foot from the chrome-hooked man’s seafoam eyes. “I take that as-a-yes”, he says. “Well, I think ya guys arr in quite a tight spot. I can get ya out of it in two ways, but I’ll onleh tell ya one. Accept your new life as a pirate, and you’ll make this a lot easier on yaselves”. The captain fiddles with his hook.
