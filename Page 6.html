Island Arrival

Your crew hastens to dock on the island, and the anchor is dropped in a semicircle bay. Most of your crew jumps overboard into the water to swim for themselves, while few others prepare a rowboat for you. “Don’t bother”, you say, as you toss your coat and captain’s hat in your quarter’s and jump over yourself. On the island, your crew gathers wood from the beach and strikes a fire. A small gathering forms at the border between the grass interior of the island to the sandy exterior. “What be the purpose of your murmuring?”, you ask as you jovially cast your arms on the shoulders of the crew beside you. One of the crew mates responds, “We were just discussing whether we should explore further into them woods there. Might be some food or animals and such. Frederick over there thinks we shouldn’t. He sides with the guy in the brig. He thinks we should just stay on the beach and leave no trace in the morn”. You look over your shoulder at a man sitting on a dead log around the fire, hunched over and rubbing his hands. Your attention is brought back to the huddle when someone else inquires “What do you think we should do, Cap’n?”.

  • Explore the woods
  • Camp on the beach. Command everyone to steer clear of the woods!
